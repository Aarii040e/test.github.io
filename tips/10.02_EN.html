INFORMATION TIP

The reality of constant access attempts

Why multi-factor authentication is an essential barrier

Hello everyone,

We often think that a work account is only interesting to attackers if it contains especially sensitive or visible information. However, access attempts are usually not personal: they are part of automated, massive and constant activity that tests credentials against thousands or millions of accounts.

These attempts can occur throughout the day and from anywhere in the world. Attackers use email databases, leaked passwords and automated tools to find any vulnerable account that may give them access to corporate information or resources.

Access attempts are constant, even when we do not see them.

The best defence against this permanent pressure is to combine strong passwords with multi-factor authentication. If someone obtains a password, the second step can prevent them from getting in.

 

Global and permanent attacks

Login attempts may come from multiple countries and time zones. They do not depend on a person manually watching an account, but on automated systems continuously testing access.
 

Data markets

There are databases containing millions of email addresses and leaked credentials. Attackers use them to test combinations and reuse passwords stolen from other services.
 

Difficulty of prosecution

Because these attacks are distributed and international, blocking or pursuing every source is very difficult. That is why prevention on each account is more effective than relying on stopping every external attempt.
 

The role of MFA

Multi-factor authentication adds an additional check. Even if an attacker obtains the password, they should not be able to sign in without the second code, notification or approval from the legitimate user.

Recommendation

When the system requests verification on your mobile phone or authentication app, do not see it as a nuisance: it is a key protection against unauthorised access. If you receive an approval request that you did not initiate, reject it immediately and report it through the established channels. That notification may mean someone knows your password and that multi-factor authentication is the last barrier.
