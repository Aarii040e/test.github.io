INFORMATION TIP

The hidden dangers of free or pirated software

If it is not official, do not install it

Hello everyone,

Downloading and installing software from unofficial websites, modified versions or pirated programs may seem like a quick way to solve a need or save costs. However, this practice creates legal, technical and operational risks for the organisation.

Unauthorised software may include malicious components, spyware, backdoors or insecure settings. A single uncontrolled installation can compromise a user’s device and become an entry point into the corporate network.

In cybersecurity, unverified “free” software can become very expensive.

Before installing any tool, it is necessary to verify its origin, legitimacy and whether its use is authorised by the organisation.

 

Legal risk

Using pirated or unlicensed software can have legal and financial consequences for the organisation and for the person who installs or uses it.
 

Software with malicious additions

Pirated or modified versions may include malware, trojans, spyware or backdoors designed to access the device or capture information.
 

Entry point into the network

A program downloaded from an untrusted source may allow an attacker to move from a single workstation to other systems, servers or corporate resources.
 

Personal responsibility

Installing unauthorised software may create responsibility if it causes a security incident, data loss or interruption of activity.

Recommendation

Install only authorised software downloaded from official channels or approved repositories. If you need a specific tool for your work, ask before downloading or installing it. Even when an application is free, always verify the developer’s reputation and avoid third-party websites, unknown installers or modified versions. Shortcuts are not worth it if they compromise everyone’s security.
