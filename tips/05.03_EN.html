Cyberattacks: a nearby reality

The arrival of mass attacks in the local environment

Hello everyone,

For a long time, cyberattacks were perceived as a distant threat, associated with large multinationals, critical infrastructure or organisations in other countries. However, incidents in recent years have shown that this perception no longer reflects reality.

Since late 2019, waves of attacks have also affected our closest environment, impacting companies, local councils and public bodies. The threat can no longer be considered external or unlikely.

Cybersecurity is a local reality.

Mass attacks have reached nearby organisations, regardless of their size, sector or whether they are public or private. The proximity of these cases shows that any organisation can become a target.

 

Waves of attacks in the Basque Country

At the end of 2019, a significant wave of attacks took place in Spain and specifically in the Basque Country, changing how many local organisations perceived cyber risk.
 

Nearby and familiar victims

Incidents affected close and recognisable entities, including industrial companies, local councils and agencies linked to public administration.
 

No distinction by size or sector

Ransomware and other threats do not only affect large corporations. They can also impact small and medium-sized companies, public bodies and private entities in any field.
 

Continuous growth

After the lockdown period, attacks have not disappeared. Their volume, frequency and aggressiveness have continued to increase, reinforcing the need to maintain a preventive attitude.

Recommendation

It is not advisable to lower our guard by assuming that an organisation is not a target. Paying attention to unexpected emails, suspicious links, unsolicited attachments or unusual behaviour can make the difference in preventing an incident.

Thank you for your cooperation.
