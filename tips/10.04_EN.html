INFORMATION TIP

Updates as shields against vulnerabilities

Why restarting is also part of security

Hello everyone,

Security updates are one of the most important measures for keeping our devices protected. All software may contain bugs or vulnerabilities that, over time, are discovered by vendors, researchers or attackers.

When a vulnerability becomes known, vendors publish updates or patches to correct it. Installing those patches helps close security gaps that could be used to access the device, run malicious code or compromise information.

A pending update is pending protection.

Every time an update is delayed, the device remains exposed to known flaws that may already be exploited by attackers.

 

Vulnerabilities as openings

Security flaws can be compared to a broken lock or an open window in our digital environment. Until they are corrected, they may allow an attacker to get in.
 

Security patches

Updates from vendors such as Microsoft fix detected vulnerabilities and strengthen the protection of the operating system, applications and installed components.
 

The necessary restart

Some updates require restarting the device to be fully applied. Although it may be inconvenient, this step is necessary for the protection to become active.
 

Risk of delay

Postponing a critical update for hours or days increases the time during which the device can be attacked through already known flaws.

Recommendation

When an update or restart notification appears, find a suitable moment to complete it as soon as possible, for example at the end of the working day, during a break or at a time when it does not interrupt a critical task. Do not ignore notifications indefinitely: the sooner the update is installed, the sooner the device recovers its level of protection.
