The rise of organised cybercrime

Why cybersecurity is more critical than ever

Hello everyone,

The current cybersecurity landscape has evolved towards an increasingly organised, professionalised and financially motivated cybercrime model. This evolution makes prevention essential to protect information, operational continuity and organisational resources.

Today’s attacks should not be seen as isolated incidents or improvised actions. In many cases, they are part of structured activity, supported by specific tools, defined objectives and a high capacity to adapt.

The threat is constant and has a real impact.

In Spain, recent data places the average number of weekly attacks per organisation close to 2,000 attempts. Although not all attempts succeed, they reflect continuous pressure on companies and institutions.

 

Financial motivation and professionalisation

Modern cybercrime is mainly driven by financial interests. Attacks may seek information theft, extortion, fraud, service disruption or direct pressure on affected organisations.

 

High technological dependence

Companies rely on systems, servers, applications and digital services to maintain daily operations. A significant failure can directly affect critical processes and business continuity.

 

Permanent attack attempts

Attack attempts occur continuously, looking for technical vulnerabilities, human errors or improper access. Security requires constant attention, not only occasional responses.

 

Social and institutional impact

The growth of cybercrime has led public institutions to reinforce specialised units to handle, investigate and provide support in relation to these incidents.

Recommendation

Security does not depend only on tools or specialised departments. Individual attention to emails, links, attachments, access requests and unexpected messages is a key barrier against a permanent threat.
