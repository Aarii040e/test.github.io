Security beyond IT: clean desk

Confidentiality is also protected at the workplace

Hello everyone,

When we talk about security, we often think of passwords, antivirus tools or cyberattacks. However, protecting information also depends on how we manage physical documents and visible material at our workstation.

A confidential document left on a desk can be seen by people passing through the area, external visitors, staff from other departments or support services. In addition, any mobile phone can now photograph exposed information in a matter of seconds.

Physical security is also information security.

A clear desk and properly stored documents reduce the risk of accidental exposure, information leakage or unauthorised access to sensitive data.

 

Physical exposure risk

Invoices, reports, customer data, employee information or strategic documentation should not remain visible when we leave the workstation or finish the working day.

 

The camera in the pocket

An exposed document can be photographed quickly without anyone noticing. An image taken in seconds may contain critical or confidential information.

 

Legal and operational impact

Information leakage in physical format can create confidentiality issues, affect the organisation and lead to legal, financial or reputational consequences.

 

Clean desk

When leaving the workstation or ending the day, sensitive information should be stored in drawers, cabinets or secure locations, preferably locked when necessary.

Recommendation

Before leaving your workstation, check that no confidential documents remain visible. Store sensitive documentation securely, remove notes containing internal information and avoid leaving files, lists, contracts or personal data on the desk. A clean desk is not just about order: it is a simple measure to protect the organisation’s confidentiality.
