The risks of USB drives and lost devices

A small device can open a major breach

Hello everyone,

USB drives and external devices may seem like simple tools for quickly moving files, but they also represent a significant security risk. They can carry malware, enable information leaks or fall outside the organisation’s control if they are lost.

Similarly, losing laptops, external drives or USB devices containing corporate information can have serious consequences, especially if the devices are not encrypted or contain personal, confidential or internal data.

An uncontrolled USB device can be an entry point or a leakage route.

The safest way to share information is to use approved corporate channels, avoiding unnecessary physical devices and always keeping assigned equipment under control.

 

Infection through external devices

A USB drive may contain malware even if it appears empty. It can also become infected when connected to compromised external computers and later bring that threat into the corporate environment.
 

Information leakage

Losing a device containing internal documents, personal data, customer information or confidential files may cause a security breach and possible legal or financial consequences.
 

Importance of encryption

If a device is not encrypted, anyone who finds or steals it may directly access the information stored on it. Encryption reduces the impact in the event of loss or theft.
 

Custody and responsibility

Assigned devices and media must be kept under control. Leaving them unattended in public places, vehicles, shared rooms or cafés increases the risk of loss, theft or unauthorised access.

Recommendation

Avoid using USB drives whenever there is a secure corporate alternative, such as approved repositories or file-sharing tools. Do not connect unknown, promotional or found USB devices. If you need to use external media, make sure it is authorised and encrypted. In case of loss, theft or suspected misuse of a device, report it immediately through the established channels.

Thank you for your cooperation.
