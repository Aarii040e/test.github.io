Payroll fraud and AI impersonation

How to protect bank account change requests

Hello everyone,

Payroll fraud is a form of impersonation in which an attacker pretends to be an employee and asks Human Resources to change the bank account used for salary payment. If the request is accepted without sufficient verification, the salary may end up in an account controlled by the criminal.

This type of fraud has evolved: it is no longer limited to email. Attackers may use phone calls, external messages and even artificial intelligence techniques to imitate voice or image and make the request appear legitimate.

Any change of bank details must be verified through a secure channel.

An apparently simple request can have a direct financial impact on the affected employee and on the organisation if it is not properly confirmed.
 

How the attack works

The attacker impersonates an employee and requests a change to the bank account used for the current month’s salary payment.
 

Warning signs

Requests sent from external email addresses, such as personal accounts, and any request involving international transfers or unusual bank details should be reviewed with special care.
 

AI-based impersonation

Fraud may rely on deepfake techniques, calls or meetings where a person’s voice or image is imitated. Therefore, an incoming call should not be considered sufficient proof unless it is verified through a reliable channel.
 

Manual verification

The most effective measure is to confirm the request directly with the person concerned through a known channel, a call initiated by the organisation or, whenever possible, an in-person procedure.

Recommendation

For any payroll bank account change request, do not rely only on the received message. Always verify the request through an independent and known channel, especially if it comes from an external account or requests an unusual account. It is better to spend one minute checking the operation than to risk diverting a salary payment.
