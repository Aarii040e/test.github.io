Antivirus as the first line of defence

The shield that must always remain active

Hello everyone,

Antivirus protection is one of the most basic and, at the same time, most important security tools for protecting our devices. Although it cannot stop 100% of threats, it helps filter a very significant part of attacks before they can run or cause damage.

Its function is not limited to the office and does not depend solely on being connected to the corporate network. Antivirus works continuously to detect suspicious files, abnormal behaviour and malicious software, both in corporate environments and on personal devices.

An active antivirus significantly reduces a device’s exposure.

Protection must always be enabled, updated and working properly. If it fails or is disabled, the device becomes much more exposed to known and automated threats.

 

Effectiveness and necessity

Antivirus does not remove all risks, but it can block a very high proportion of common threats. Without this layer of protection, a connected device is much more exposed from the very beginning.
 

Protection at home too

Security does not end in the workplace. Personal devices should also have active protection. On Windows, Microsoft Defender is built in and should remain enabled and updated.
 

Continuous operation

Antivirus can continue protecting the device even when it is not connected to the corporate network, VPN or remote access tools. Local protection remains necessary at all times.
 

Warnings and incidents

If the antivirus shows errors, generates alerts, appears disabled or does not update correctly, it must be reported immediately so that protection can be reviewed and restored.

Recommendation

Do not disable antivirus protection or ignore its warnings. If an error message, threat alert or any sign that protection is not working properly appears, report it through the established channels. Keeping antivirus active and updated is one of the simplest ways to prevent common threats from compromising the device and, potentially, the organisation’s network.
