Why simple deletion is not enough

Deleting a file does not always mean deleting the data

Hello everyone,

We often think that deleting a file, emptying the recycle bin or quickly formatting a USB drive makes the information disappear forever. However, in many cases the content may still remain on the device and be recoverable with relatively simple tools.

This risk is especially important when dealing with USB drives, hard drives, memory cards, mobile phones or any media that has contained internal information, personal data, customer data, payroll information or confidential documentation.

Deleting does not always mean destroying the information.

Simple deletion may hide the file from the user, but it does not guarantee that the data has physically disappeared from the storage media.

 

The deletion myth

Deleting a file usually removes the visible reference, but the data may remain stored until it is overwritten. Therefore, apparently empty media may still contain recoverable information.

 

Secure deletion or physical destruction

To remove information definitively, specific secure deletion tools must be used or physical destruction of the media must be applied when appropriate.

 

Mobile devices

Before reassigning a corporate phone to another person, a full and controlled factory reset must be performed to prevent data, accounts, files or settings from the previous user from remaining.

 

Professional handling

Any device that has contained sensitive information must be handed over to the responsible area for proper treatment, rather than being thrown away, given away, reused or disposed of without control.

Recommendation

Do not throw away or reuse USB drives, disks, memory cards, mobile phones or other media that have contained corporate information without prior validation. Hand them over to the appropriate department so that secure deletion, physical destruction or the correct procedure can be performed. In security, definitive deletion requires technical control, not just pressing “delete”.
