The 30-second rule for locking your session

Protect your device every time you leave your desk

Hello everyone,

Locking your session when leaving your workstation is one of the simplest and most effective security measures we can apply every day. Although it may seem like a small gesture, leaving a computer open and unattended can allow unauthorised access to information, applications or corporate documents.

This is not about distrusting colleagues, but about protecting the work environment. In offices, shared rooms or transit areas, external staff, visitors or people from other departments may pass by. An unlocked device makes it easier for someone to act under the user’s identity with little trace.

An unlocked computer can become a risk in seconds.

Locking the session does not close documents or applications. It simply prevents another person from using the device while the user is away.

 

Immediate vulnerability

In a very short time, a malicious person could access information, send emails, copy files or try to install malicious software on an unlocked device.

 

Shared environment

Even when there is internal trust, workstations may be exposed to visitors, suppliers, external staff or people from other departments. An open session leaves the device uncontrolled.

 

Instant lock

In Windows, the Windows + L shortcut locks the screen immediately without closing anything that is open. It is a quick gesture that barely interrupts work.

 

Easy return

When returning to the workstation, simply enter the password or use the configured authentication method to continue exactly where the session was left.

Recommendation

Get used to pressing Windows + L every time you leave your workstation, even if it is only for a moment. Turning session locking into an automatic habit prevents unauthorised access and protects both your information and the organisation’s information. One second of prevention can avoid a much more serious incident.
