INFORMATION TIP

The safest computer is the one that is switched off

Shutting down also protects the network

Hello everyone,

At the end of the working day, we often leave the computer on out of convenience or habit. However, fully shutting down the device when it will not be used is a simple measure that reduces exposure to unauthorised remote access.

Attackers who manage to enter a network often try to move silently at night, during weekends or during periods of low activity. If devices are switched on, they can become available points for moving further, searching for information or attempting to access other systems.

A switched-off device limits an attacker’s movement options.

If the computer is not powered on or available on the network, the attack surface is reduced and it becomes harder to use it as a foothold during an intrusion.

 

Blocking attacker movement

During an intrusion, an attacker may try to jump from one device to another to expand access. Shutting down devices outside working hours reduces the number of available targets.

 

Connection interruption

A switched-off computer is no longer accessible for normal remote connections. This makes silent movement through the network more difficult at night or during weekends.

 

Energy saving

In addition to improving security, shutting down devices helps reduce electricity consumption and supports more responsible environmental practices.

 

Simple measure

Shutting down the device at the end of the day does not require technical knowledge or additional tools. It is a simple habit with a direct impact on security and sustainability.

Recommendation

At the end of the working day or before a weekend, fully shut down your computer whenever there is no justified need to keep it running. Avoid leaving it only suspended if you are not going to use it. This gesture reduces the device’s exposure, makes attacker movement more difficult and contributes to energy saving.

Thank you for your cooperation.
